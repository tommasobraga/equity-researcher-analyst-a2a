# Apple Inc. — Annual Report on Form 10-K
**Ticker:** AAPL | **Doc-type:** 10-K | **Fiscal Year:** FY2025 (ended September 27, 2025)
**Source:** SEC EDGAR | **Classification:** Public

---

## Item 1. Business

Apple Inc. designs, manufactures, and markets smartphones, personal computers, tablets, wearables, and accessories worldwide, and sells a variety of related services. The Company's fiscal year ends on the last Saturday of September.

### Products

**iPhone** — Revenue FY2025: $218.4B (+6% YoY). iPhone 17 Pro series launched September 2025 with Apple Intelligence integration at hardware level. Average selling price increased to $946 (+4% YoY) driven by Pro mix shift.

**Mac** — Revenue FY2025: $37.2B (+12% YoY). M4 family transition substantially complete. Mac revenue benefited from enterprise refresh cycles driven by on-device AI capabilities.

**iPad** — Revenue FY2025: $28.6B (+8% YoY).

**Wearables, Home and Accessories** — Revenue FY2025: $41.8B (-2% YoY).

**Services** — Revenue FY2025: $117.4B (+15% YoY). Gross margin: 75.1%. App Store, Apple Music, iCloud+, Apple TV+, Apple Arcade, Apple Pay, AppleCare. Monthly active paying subscribers: 1.12 billion (+13% YoY).

**Total Net Sales FY2025: $443.4B** (+7% YoY)

### Apple Intelligence

Apple Intelligence, launched with iOS 18.1 / macOS Sequoia in October 2024 and expanded to all markets by FY2025 Q2, represents our integrated approach to on-device AI. Key features include Writing Tools, Priority Inbox, Image Playground, and Siri with enhanced contextual awareness. All processing occurs on-device or through Private Cloud Compute — no user data is logged on Apple servers.

As of September 2025, Apple Intelligence is available on iPhone 15 Pro and later (approximately 320M devices in the installed base).

## Item 1A. Risk Factors

**Services Growth Sustainability.** The App Store faces regulatory scrutiny across multiple jurisdictions. The EU Digital Markets Act required Apple to allow alternative app distribution and browser engines in the EU from March 2024. We estimate a revenue impact of approximately $2.3B annually from EU DMA compliance. Similar legislation is advancing in the UK, Japan, and the US.

**China Risk.** Greater China revenue of $68.4B declined 4% YoY in FY2025, reflecting intensified competition from Huawei following the launch of the Mate 60 Pro series and ongoing regulatory scrutiny of US technology in China. iPhone market share in China declined from 19% (FY2024) to approximately 17% (FY2025).

**AI Competitive Positioning.** Our on-device AI approach differs from cloud-based competitors (Google Gemini, Microsoft Copilot). While we believe Private Cloud Compute offers superior privacy, market acceptance of this approach and its impact on long-term Services monetisation remains uncertain.

## Item 7. Management's Discussion and Analysis

### Gross Margin

| Segment | FY2025 Revenue | Gross Margin |
|---|---|---|
| Products | $326.0B | 37.2% |
| Services | $117.4B | 75.1% |
| **Total** | **$443.4B** | **47.1%** |

Total gross margin of 47.1% represents a 110bps improvement YoY, driven by Services mix shift and iPhone 17 Pro pricing power.

### Capital Return Programme

Apple returned $112.4B to shareholders in FY2025: $90.1B in share repurchases (reducing share count 3.4% YoY) and $22.3B in dividends ($1.00 per share quarterly, +5% increase announced February 2025). The Board approved an incremental $120B repurchase authorisation in May 2025.

Free cash flow: $118.3B. Net cash position: -$52.3B (net debt, reflecting aggressive buyback activity).

### FY2026 Outlook

Management does not provide formal guidance. Consensus expectations (Bloomberg) call for FY2026 revenue of approximately $468B (+6% YoY), driven by continued Services growth and iPhone 18 cycle optimism. iPhone 18 is expected to include the first Apple-designed cellular modem, reducing dependence on Qualcomm.
