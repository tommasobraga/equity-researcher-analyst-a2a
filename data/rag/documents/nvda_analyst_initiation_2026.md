## Investment Thesis

We initiate coverage of NVIDIA Corporation with an OVERWEIGHT rating and a $165 price target, implying 32% upside. We believe the market underestimates the durability of NVIDIA's earnings power in a world where AI infrastructure spending is transitioning from a build phase to a monetisation phase.

**Three pillars of our thesis:**

**1. Inference as the next growth vector.** Training demand was the FY2024–2025 story. Inference — running deployed AI models in production — will be the FY2026–2028 story. Inference workloads favour GPUs due to low-latency requirements and the dominance of transformer architectures. We estimate the inference TAM at $95B by 2028, up from $22B in 2025. NVIDIA's NIM (NVIDIA Inference Microservices) platform reduces deployment friction and creates a recurring software revenue stream we estimate at $4–6B by FY2028.

**2. CUDA moat is structural, not cyclical.** 5.5 million registered CUDA developers represent a network effect that compounds. Every enterprise AI engineer trained on PyTorch/CUDA raises the switching cost for the next one. AMD's ROCm ecosystem, while improving, lags on library coverage (approximately 65% of CUDA-optimised workloads have validated ROCm equivalents vs. 100% on CUDA). We model NVIDIA maintaining 75–80% GPU compute market share through 2028.

**3. Sovereigns and enterprise as incremental demand.** Hyperscaler capex is the consensus driver. The under-appreciated story is sovereign AI — national AI infrastructure programmes in France (€1.5B), Germany (€5B via SPRIND), UAE (Stargate), Saudi Arabia (Project Transcendence), and India. We estimate sovereign demand at $18–22B over 2026–2028. Enterprise direct purchasing (not through cloud resellers) is also accelerating as companies seek on-premise AI for data sovereignty.

## Key Risks

**Bear case — hyperscaler digestion.** If the top-5 hyperscalers pause GPU orders for 2–3 quarters to absorb installed capacity (as occurred in 2022–23 with data centre servers), quarterly revenue could decline 15–20% from peak. We assign 25% probability to a digestion scenario materialising in H2 2026.

**Custom silicon displacement.** Google's TPU v5, Amazon Trainium 2, and Microsoft Maia accelerate. We model these taking 8–12 percentage points of internal workload share at the three hyperscalers by FY2028, displacing approximately $12–15B in NVIDIA revenue at full displacement.

**Export controls.** A further tightening of US export controls to cover the A100-equivalent performance threshold would reduce our China revenue estimate by $4B/year.
