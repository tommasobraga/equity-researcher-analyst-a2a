# UniCredit S.p.A. — Q4 and Full Year 2025 Earnings Call Transcript
**Ticker:** UCG.MI | **Doc-type:** Earnings Call Transcript | **Period:** Q4 2025 / FY2025
**Date:** February 5, 2026 | **Exchange:** Borsa Italiana (Euronext Milan) | **Classification:** Public

---

## Opening Remarks — CEO Andrea Orcel

"Good morning. I am pleased to report another year of record performance. Full year 2025 net profit of €10.8 billion represents a 14% increase year-over-year, the third consecutive year of record profits since we began our transformation in 2022.

Our return on tangible equity stands at 20.1% for FY2025. This is not a cyclical peak — it reflects structural improvements in our capital efficiency, cost base, and revenue quality. Net interest income of €15.2 billion was essentially flat year-over-year as we managed the transition from the ECB rate tightening cycle to the current easing environment, while fee income grew 9% to €7.4 billion, demonstrating the quality of our client franchise.

We have deployed capital aggressively and responsibly. The €8.6 billion capital distribution for FY2025 — comprising €4.8 billion in buybacks and €3.8 billion in dividends — represents a total yield of approximately 13% at current market prices. We have committed to a minimum of €9.0 billion in distributions for FY2026.

On Commerzbank: we have now crossed the 30% economic interest threshold. Regulatory approvals in Germany are progressing. We remain confident in the strategic and financial rationale. The combined entity would create Europe's premier corporate and investment bank."

## CFO Remarks — Stefano Porro

**Net Interest Income (NII):** €15.2B FY2025 vs €15.4B FY2024. NII compression was less than guided due to faster repricing of our loan book in Germany and Central Eastern Europe (CEE). CEE remains a structural NII contributor at approximately 28% of group NII.

**Fees and Commissions:** €7.4B (+9% YoY). Wealth management and protection products drove the growth, with AUM increasing to €258B (+12% YoY) driven by net inflows of €18B and market appreciation.

**Loan Loss Provisions (LLP):** Cost of risk of 14 basis points, below the 15–17bps guidance range. NPE ratio declined to 2.9% (from 3.6% at end-2022), gross NPE stock at €13.7B.

**Capital:** CET1 ratio 16.2% (fully loaded). MDA buffer of 630 basis points over minimum requirements. SREP requirement unchanged at 10.42%.

## Q&A Highlights

**Q (Barclays):** What is the sensitivity of NII to a 25bps ECB rate cut?

**A (CFO):** Our NII sensitivity to -25bps is approximately -€350M annualised at current deposit pass-through levels. We have partially hedged this through a structural fixed-rate bond portfolio of approximately €95B with an average duration of 2.8 years. We expect to fully offset the NII headwind from easing through volume growth and fee expansion by H2 2026.

**Q (JPMorgan):** On Commerzbank — can you confirm the timeline for a formal public offer?

**A (CEO):** We continue to engage constructively with the German government and Commerzbank management. We expect to have regulatory clarity on our stake in Q2 2026. A formal offer decision would follow that clarity. I will not speculate on timing beyond that.

**Q (Goldman Sachs):** Is the 20%+ RoTE sustainable post-rate normalisation?

**A (CEO):** Yes. Our structural cost efficiency (cost/income ratio of 37.4% in FY2025), diversified revenue mix, and capital discipline provide the foundation for sustainable returns above our cost of equity regardless of the rate environment. We are not a monoline NII story.
