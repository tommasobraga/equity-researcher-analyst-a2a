## Business Overview

ASML is the world's only supplier of extreme ultraviolet (EUV) lithography systems and the leading supplier of deep ultraviolet (DUV) systems. Our machines are essential to the production of virtually all advanced semiconductors. Without ASML technology, modern chip manufacturing at nodes below 7nm is not possible.

### Product Lines

**EUV (Extreme Ultraviolet)** — TWINSCAN NXE and NXE:3800 systems for leading-edge logic (5nm, 3nm, 2nm) and advanced DRAM. Average selling price: approximately €180–200M per system. Delivery lead time: 18–24 months.

**High-NA EUV** — TWINSCAN EXE:5000 (NA=0.55), production start 2024. Required for 2nm and beyond (gate-all-around, backside power delivery). Only TSMC, Samsung, and Intel have placed orders as of end FY2025. ASP: approximately €350–380M per system.

**DUV (Deep Ultraviolet)** — TWINSCAN NXT series, ArFi immersion systems for mature and leading-edge nodes. Subject to Dutch export controls restricting sales to China effective September 2023. DUV sales to China declined from 49% of DUV revenue in FY2022 to approximately 12% in FY2025.

## Risk Factors

**Export Controls — China.** The Netherlands government has restricted exports of certain DUV systems (TWINSCAN NXT:2000i and above) to China effective September 2023. Approximately 25 export licences for previously ordered systems were revoked. We estimate revenue impact of €3.5–4.0B per annum relative to an unrestricted scenario. Further restrictions on service and spare parts for already-installed systems in China remain possible.

**Customer Concentration.** Three customers — TSMC, Samsung, and Intel — accounted for approximately 78% of net revenue in FY2025. A capacity digestion pause at any of these customers (as occurred in 2023) creates revenue lumpiness despite our backlog.

**High-NA EUV Ramp Risk.** The EXE:5000 is a fundamentally new platform. Customer qualification, yield learning, and resist performance at High-NA are all in early stages. Any delays in customer ramp would defer revenue recognition under IFRS 15 performance obligation criteria.

## Strategic Outlook

Management reconfirmed the 2030 target model: revenue of €44–60B, gross margin of 56–60%. The wide range reflects uncertainty around the pace of High-NA adoption, China exposure, and memory vs. logic spending mix.

EUV installed base productivity (wafers per day per tool) continues to improve through in-field upgrades, generating recurring service revenue (approximately 27% of total FY2025 revenue).

**Dividend and Buyback.** Total capital returned to shareholders in FY2025: €11.8B (€6.4B buybacks + €5.4B dividends). Buyback programme of €2.5B per quarter ongoing through FY2026 Q2.
