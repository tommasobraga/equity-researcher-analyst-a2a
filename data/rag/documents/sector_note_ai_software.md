# Sector Note: AI & Enterprise Software
**Doc-type:** Sector Note | **Date:** April 2026 | **Classification:** Internal Use Only
**Analyst:** Research Desk EMEA Tech

---

## Sector View: POSITIVE with increasing selectivity

The AI infrastructure investment cycle has entered the monetisation phase. After two years of aggressive hyperscaler capex, the market now demands evidence of measurable returns. Enterprise software companies integrating AI into workflows are emerging as the primary indirect beneficiaries.

---

## Key Themes Q2 2026

### 1. Copilot Monetisation Inflection
Microsoft 365 Copilot (MSFT) has crossed the 2.1M enterprise seat threshold, with a $30/user/month price point representing a 25% premium over the base M365 E3 licence. At scale, Copilot could add $5–7B incremental revenue by FY2027. The key question is seat growth beyond early adopters — enterprise IT budget cycles take 12–18 months.

Competitor landscape: Google Workspace Duet AI at $30/user/month comparable pricing. Salesforce Einstein AI embedded in core CRM (no standalone pricing). SAP Business AI in BTP: €0.12/document processed.

### 2. AI-Native vs. AI-Bolted
The market is bifurcating between companies that are AI-native (ServiceNow, Palantir, Snowflake) and those bolting AI onto legacy products. Our preference is for AI-native companies where AI is core to the value proposition rather than a feature addition. In our coverage universe, MSFT falls into the "deep integration" category — Azure AI is foundational, not additive.

### 3. SaaS Renewal Cycle
FY2023–2024 SaaS correction (multiple compression, seat count rationalisation post-COVID) is largely resolved. Net Revenue Retention (NRR) metrics are recovering across the enterprise software sector. MSFT Dynamics NRR estimated at 115% in FY2025, up from 108% in FY2023.

## Company Views

**Microsoft (MSFT) — BUY, PT $535**

- Copilot monetisation adds optionality not fully priced by consensus
- AI capex ($80B+ commitment through FY2026) is a risk but also a barrier to entry
- Key risk: OpenAI relationship deterioration or regulatory restriction

**Risks to sector view**
- AI pricing pressure: if Azure and competing clouds lower AI API pricing materially (as occurred with standard compute), gross margin compression could follow
- Enterprise budget fatigue: CIOs are managing multiple AI vendor relationships; concentration is likely, favouring incumbents
- EU AI Act enforcement from August 2026 may create a deployment pause in regulated industries
