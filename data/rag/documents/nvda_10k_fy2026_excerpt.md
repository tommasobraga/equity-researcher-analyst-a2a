## Item 1. Business

NVIDIA pioneered accelerated computing to help solve the most challenging computational problems. We are a full-stack computing company with data center-scale offerings that are reshaping industry. Our GPU platform is the de facto standard for AI training and inference workloads globally.

### Segments

**Data Center** — GPU compute and networking for AI, high-performance computing, and cloud infrastructure. Revenue FY2026: $115.3B (+141% YoY). Blackwell GPU architecture deployed at scale from Q3 FY2026 with 400,000+ H200 and B200 units shipped.

**Gaming** — GeForce GPUs for PC gaming and console SoCs. Revenue FY2026: $13.2B (+8% YoY).

**Professional Visualization** — Workstation GPUs. Revenue FY2026: $2.6B (+14% YoY).

**Automotive** — DRIVE platform for autonomous vehicles. Revenue FY2026: $1.7B (+47% YoY), driven by DRIVE Thor adoption at Mercedes-Benz and BYD.

**OEM and Other** — $1.1B.

**Total Revenue FY2026: $133.9B** (+122% YoY)

### CUDA Ecosystem Moat

Over 5.5 million developers have registered on the NVIDIA developer platform as of January 2026. The CUDA software stack, comprising more than 600 optimized libraries and 4,000+ GPU-accelerated applications, represents a switching cost that we believe is unmatched in the industry. Replicating the CUDA ecosystem is estimated by independent analysts to require 5–10 years and tens of billions of dollars.

### Blackwell Architecture

The Blackwell GPU delivers up to 4× the training throughput and 30× the inference performance of its predecessor (Hopper) for transformer-based models. The GB200 NVL72 rack-scale system, comprising 36 Grace CPUs and 72 Blackwell GPUs in a NVLink interconnect, is the primary platform for hyperscaler AI clusters. Lead time for GB200 systems: 52 weeks as of fiscal year-end.

## Item 1A. Risk Factors

**Export Controls.** US export regulations restrict our ability to sell certain GPU products to China and other countries. The A800/H800 export-compliant variants generated approximately $12.4B in China revenue in FY2025 before the October 2023 restrictions. As of FY2026, China revenue from Data Center has declined to approximately $4.2B. Future regulatory changes remain a material risk.

**Customer Concentration.** Our top five customers (Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Meta, Oracle) collectively accounted for approximately 46% of Data Center revenue in FY2026. Loss of any single hyperscaler relationship would be material.

**Manufacturing Concentration.** Substantially all of our products are manufactured by TSMC. Any disruption to TSMC's operations — including natural disasters, geopolitical events, or power constraints in Taiwan — would materially impact supply.

**Competitive Threat.** AMD MI300X and Intel Gaudi 3 are gaining traction in inference workloads. In-house silicon from Google (TPU), Amazon (Trainium), and Microsoft (Maia) may reduce GPU demand at the margin over the medium term.
