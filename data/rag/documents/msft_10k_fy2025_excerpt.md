# Microsoft Corporation — Annual Report on Form 10-K
**Ticker:** MSFT | **Doc-type:** 10-K | **Fiscal Year:** FY2025 (ended June 30, 2025)
**Source:** SEC EDGAR | **Classification:** Public

---

## Item 1. Business

Microsoft Corporation enables digital transformation for the era of an intelligent cloud and an intelligent edge. Our mission is to empower every person and every organization on the planet to achieve more.

We generate revenue by developing, licensing, and supporting a wide range of software products, services, devices, and solutions. We also design, manufacture, and sell devices and we deliver relevant online advertising to a global audience.

### Segments

**Productivity and Business Processes** — Office Commercial products and cloud services, LinkedIn, and Dynamics. Revenue FY2025: $82.4B (+12% YoY).

**Intelligent Cloud** — Server products and cloud services, including Azure. Revenue FY2025: $135.6B (+29% YoY). Azure and other cloud services grew 35% in constant currency. GitHub Copilot enterprise seats surpassed 2.1 million as of Q4 FY2025.

**More Personal Computing** — Windows, Devices, Gaming, and Search. Revenue FY2025: $51.8B (+4% YoY).

### AI Strategy

We have integrated AI across our product portfolio through Copilot, powered by models developed in partnership with OpenAI. Microsoft 365 Copilot is now deployed in over 85% of Fortune 500 companies. Azure OpenAI Service processed more than 10 trillion tokens per month as of June 2025.

We have committed over $80 billion in AI infrastructure capital expenditure through FY2026, the majority of which will be deployed in the United States and Europe.

## Item 1A. Risk Factors

**Competition.** We face intense competition across all segments. In cloud infrastructure, Amazon Web Services and Google Cloud Platform represent our primary competitors. Azure's market share in enterprise workloads has expanded to approximately 25% globally, but price competition remains significant, particularly in commodity compute.

**AI Regulatory Risk.** The EU AI Act (effective August 2024, enforcement from August 2026) classifies certain AI systems as high-risk and imposes conformity assessment obligations. Microsoft's Copilot products in healthcare and financial services verticals may be subject to enhanced compliance requirements, potentially increasing operating costs.

**OpenAI Dependency.** Our Copilot products depend on continued access to OpenAI models. Any deterioration in the commercial relationship, adverse regulatory action against OpenAI, or material changes to the API terms could adversely affect our AI product roadmap.

**Cybersecurity.** In Q2 FY2025, we disclosed a nation-state actor breach of Exchange Online email accounts belonging to several US government agencies. We have undertaken a Secure Future Initiative with an estimated incremental cost of $3.5B over three years.

### Capital Allocation

- Share repurchases: $17.8B in FY2025
- Dividends paid: $9.3B ($2.48 per share, +10% YoY)
- Free cash flow: $73.6B ($9.85/share)

### Cloud Gross Margin

Azure gross margin improved 180bps YoY to 72.4%, driven by improved server utilization and the transition to custom silicon (Maia AI accelerator deployed at scale from Q3 FY2025).

### Outlook

For FY2026 Q1, management guided revenue of $68.1–69.1B, implying approximately 14–16% YoY growth. Azure growth guided at 34–35% in constant currency, accelerating from Q4 FY2025 driven by AI workload expansion.
